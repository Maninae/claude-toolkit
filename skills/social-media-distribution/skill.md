# Social Media Distribution Strategy

## Overview

When you have a blog post, project writeup, or long-form content to share across platforms, each platform requires different formatting, timing, and link placement to maximize genuine engagement. This skill covers the tactical playbook.

**Core principle:** Give away the good stuff natively on each platform. Don't write teasers — share 2-3 real standalone insights. People who find them valuable will click through to the full post.

## Distribution Order (Same Day)

| Step | Platform | Time | Format | Link Placement |
|------|----------|------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Threads | Wed/Thu 9am | 3-5 post thread | Last post or reply |
| 2 | LinkedIn | Same day 3-4pm | Standalone text post | First comment |
| 3 | Hacker News | Same/next day morning | "Show HN" title | Direct link |
| 4 | Reddit | Same/next day | Text post + summary | In post body |

**Don't spread across multiple days** — launch energy dissipates. Same 24-48 hour window.

## Threads

**Character limits:**
- 500 chars per post (text body)
- Text attachments up to 10,000 chars (don't count toward 500)
- Images supported and outperform text-only

**Format: Multi-post thread (3-5 posts)**

- **Post 1 (hook):** Concise, genuine statement. Under 400 chars. NO link. Ask a question or make a specific claim that invites response.
- **Posts 2-4:** Pull 2-3 most interesting/surprising insights from the content. Each should stand alone as valuable. "Give away the good stuff for free."
- **Final post:** The link, framed as "full deep-dive here." Isolating the link avoids the algorithmic penalty.

**Why this structure:**
- Long-form threads average ~4,400 views per post (highest reach of any format)
- Posts with links get 30-50% less reach — keep links out of early posts
- The algorithm heavily rewards genuine reply conversations (10 reply chains > 100 passive likes)

**Timing:** Wednesday or Thursday morning, 7am-12pm local time. Wednesday strongest overall. Avoid weekends.

**Tone:** Conversational, informal, peer-to-peer. Write like you're talking to a friend at a meetup, not writing a press release.

**Don't:**
- Post everything at once as one massive text dump
- Make it sound promotional
- Use carousels unless you have genuinely compelling visuals

## LinkedIn

**Format: Standalone text post (1,300-1,900 characters)**

NOT a link post. External links reduce LinkedIn reach by 30-60%. The "link in first comment" workaround is also penalized as of early 2026, but still less than in-body links.

**Post structure:**
- **First 210-235 characters are critical** — this is all that shows before "See more." 60-70% of readers are lost at this decision point. Make it count.
- 1,300-1,900 chars total (~200-300 words)
- Use generous line breaks — dense paragraphs kill engagement
- End with a specific question (not generic "what do you think?" — something like "what's the most useful thing your AI setup does that you didn't expect?")

**Link placement options (pick one):**
1. First comment (still penalized, but less than in-body)
2. "Link in my profile" / Featured section
3. Mention the URL in text without making it a hyperlink

**Alternative format — Document carousel (PDF swipe):**
- Highest engagement rate on LinkedIn (6.6%)
- Works great for architecture diagrams, system overviews, visual walkthroughs
- Create a 5-10 slide PDF with key visuals + captions
- Use this if you have strong diagrams

**Timing:** Wednesday afternoon 3-4pm, or Friday afternoon. Late afternoon/evening outperforms traditional morning business hours now.

**For technical/AI content:** Niche content actually performs better — the algorithm identifies topics and serves to the right audience. Write for 50 engaged practitioners, not 5,000 passive scrollers.

## Hacker News

**Format: "Show HN: [Title]" — direct link to blog post**

- HN expects and rewards direct links (unlike Threads/LinkedIn)
- Title should be descriptive and specific, not clickbaity
- Good: "Show HN: I built a personal AI assistant with memory, recurring jobs, and a 3-layer security firewall"
- Bad: "My AI assistant is incredible — here's how I built it"

**Timing:** Weekday mornings US Eastern, or Sunday 11am-12pm UTC.

**Expectations:** HN is luck-dependent for small accounts. Posts sometimes flop on first submission and succeed months later. Don't be discouraged. You can resubmit (HN allows this).

**If it gets traction:** Be in the comments answering questions. HN rewards engaged OPs.

## Reddit

**Target subreddits:**
- **r/ClaudeAI** — Most relevant if system uses Claude. Collaborative community, appreciates practical builds.
- **r/LocalLLaMA** — If system involves local/open models
- **r/artificial** — General AI agent architecture

**Format:** Text post with summary + link in body. NOT a link-only post.

**Structure:**
- Title: specific and descriptive
- Body: 3-5 paragraph summary of what you built and what you learned
- Link to full blog post at the end
- Tone: humble, genuine, specific. Reddit communities hate self-promotion. Lead with substance.

## Platform Skip List

- **Twitter/X:** Low ROI for small accounts in 2026, too much noise
- **Facebook:** Wrong audience
- **Medium:** Paywalled, frustrating for readers

## Cross-Platform Principles

1. **Each platform gets native content** — don't copy-paste the same text everywhere
2. **Extract 2-3 standalone insights** that are valuable even without clicking through
3. **Match the platform's tone** — casual on Threads, professional on LinkedIn, technical on HN, genuine on Reddit
4. **Links are poison on social feeds** — isolate them (last in thread, in comments, etc.)
5. **Ask real questions** — algorithms track replies, not just likes
6. **Post once, well** — don't spam multiple times hoping for traction
